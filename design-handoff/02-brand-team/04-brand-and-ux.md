# HormonaIQ — Brand, UX & Design System
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 26, 2026  
**Design Philosophy:** Companion, not clinic. Campaign, not app.

---

## THE NORTH STAR

> **HormonaIQ should feel like receiving a text from a close friend who happens to have a PhD in women's health.**

It is not a health app. It is not a tracker. It is not a medical tool.  
It is a **companion that understands you** — and proactively shows up for you.

Every design decision flows from one question:  
**"Does this feel like a friend, or does it feel like a hospital form?"**

---

## BRAND IDENTITY

### The Name
**HormonaIQ** — intelligence, not just tracking. The "IQ" signals that this is not passive logging; it's the app thinking with you, connecting dots you can't see.

### Brand Personality (The 5 Words)
1. **Refreshing** — light, clean, breathable, not heavy or clinical
2. **Honest** — no toxic positivity, no "you're LUMINOUS today!" when you feel like death
3. **Warm** — safe, seen, held — like the best friend who just gets it
4. **Sharp** — intelligent, not dumbed down — women are sophisticated, treat them that way
5. **Brave** — willing to name the hard things (suicidal ideation, hormonal rage, medical gaslighting)

### What This Is NOT
- ❌ Not a fertility app (this is the single biggest mistake competitors make)
- ❌ Not clinical (no doctor-white, no hospital-green, no cold sterile interfaces)
- ❌ Not a period tracker (period tracking is one input, not the product)
- ❌ Not a wellness app (no crystals, no "manifest your best self", no toxic positivity)
- ❌ Not a diagnostic tool (we validate, we don't diagnose — legal and ethical necessity)

## VISUAL IDENTITY

### The Feeling — Reference Brands
Design at the intersection of these three worlds:
- **Apple Health** — clarity, breathing room, data that feels intelligent not clinical
- **Headspace** — warmth, soft illustration, emotional safety in UI
- **Notion/Linear** — clean, thoughtful typography, professional without being corporate

We are building the **Apple of women's hormonal health** — not the cheapest, not the clinical, but the most trusted, most beautiful, most intelligent version.

**Phase Color System (The Core Innovation)**

This is how we map emotional states to color without alarming users:

| Phase | Color | Psychology |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Follicular** (Days 1-13) | `#C5E0C3` (soft sage) | Fresh start, possibility |
| **Ovulation** (Day 14 ±2) | `#EDD89A` (warm gold) | Energy peak, but no toxic hype |
| **Luteal Early** (Days 15-21) | `#C5BDE0` (soft lavender) | Heads up, shifting |
| **Luteal Peak / PMDD** (Days 22-28) | `#B8AAD4` (deeper lavender) | Acknowledged darkness — not alarming |
| **Menstruation** | `#F2CFC9` (blush soft) | Release, relief, rest |

The insight: we don't use **red for period** (alarm) or **pink for ovulation** (fertility framing). We use a natural progression that maps to feeling, not fertility.

### Typography

**Primary Typeface: Inter (or SF Pro on iOS)**  
Why: Maximum legibility. Warm but professional. Apple-tier baseline.

**Secondary Typeface: Fraunces (serif, for emotional moments)**  
Why: Adds warmth and weight for headlines, pull quotes, affirmations. Signals "this matters."

**Type Scale:**

| Name | Size | Weight | Use |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| Display | 32px | 600 | Screen headers, emotional moments |
| Heading 1 | 24px | 600 | Section titles |
| Heading 2 | 20px | 600 | Card titles |
| Body Large | 17px | 400 | Primary reading content |
| Body | 15px | 400 | Standard UI text |
| Body Small | 13px | 400 | Supporting text |
| Caption | 11px | 500 | Labels, timestamps |

**Writing Rule:** All copy is written in second person, conversational, present tense.  
❌ "User has entered luteal phase"  
✓ "Heads up — you're heading into luteal."

## THE LOGO

### Concept Direction

The logo should feel like it was designed by a small, intentional studio — not a startup template factory.

**Core Concept: The Cycle Mark**  
A continuous loop — but not a perfect circle. Slightly organic, slightly imperfect. Like a human hand drawing it. It represents: cyclical time, the continuous nature of hormonal life, and the idea that every phase is connected.

**Logo Mark Options to Explore:**

**Option A — The Crescent Wave**  
A flowing line that curves like both a crescent moon and a wave. Suggests cyclical change, tidal rhythms (hormones are tidal), and movement. Clean, minimal. Works at any size.

**Option B — The Leaf Loop**  
An abstract shape that is simultaneously a leaf (natural, organic) and a loop (cyclical). The negative space inside creates a soft organic window. Sage green.

**Option C — The Overlapping Phases**  
Three overlapping soft circles, each slightly translucent, in follicular/luteal/menstruation colors. Suggests phases coexisting, the complexity of hormonal experience, and the "intelligence" in the name.

**Recommended Direction: Option A or C**  
Option A for maximum versatility (works small, works on dark/light)  
Option C for maximum meaning (but more complex)

**Logo Don'ts:**
- ❌ No uterus/ovary imagery (clinical, alienating to non-cis users)
- ❌ No drop of blood (too literal, too alarming)
- ❌ No generic woman silhouette (generic, 2015)
- ❌ No pink gradient (Flo aesthetic — differentiate)

**Wordmark:**  
"HormonaIQ" — the "IQ" can be subtly differentiated (slightly bolder, or in the accent sage) to signal intelligence without screaming it.

**Color Application:**  
Primary: Sage `#8DAF8A` on Cloud White `#FAFAF9`  
Dark mode: Cloud White on `#1C1A18`  
Minimal mode: Text Primary only (no color) for serious contexts

## UX ARCHITECTURE

### The Core Experience Principle: Proactive Companionship

The app does not wait for users to log. It reaches out.

**The 3 Modes of Proactive Interaction:**

**1. Phase Awareness (Always-On)**  
The app always knows where you are in your cycle and adjusts every surface accordingly:
- App icon badge changes subtly with phase (not alarming, just present)
- Home screen header changes tone based on phase
- Notification timing adjusts (don't send high-demand tasks during luteal peak)

**2. Pattern Recognition (Intelligent)**  
After 2-3 cycles of data, the app starts making predictions:
- "Last month you had 3 days of brain fog around day 22. Tomorrow is day 21."
- "Your sleep usually disrupts 2 days before your period. Tonight might be hard."
- These are not warnings. They are informed companionship.

**3. Proactive Outreach (Relational)**  
The app checks in, not in a clinical way:
- "How are you feeling right now?" (not "log your symptoms")
- "You haven't logged in 3 days — no pressure, just here when you need us."
- 3AM notification option: "Can't sleep? You're not alone." (optional, user-activated)

### Navigation Architecture

**5 Core Spaces (Bottom Nav):**

```
[Today]  [Cycle]  [Log]  [Insights]  [You]
```

**Today** — The companion hub. Phase-aware greeting. What's likely today. One-tap check-in. Community pulse (anonymous, "X others are in luteal today"). 

**Cycle** — The calendar/ring view. Not a period tracker — a life map. Shows all phases, patterns, predictions. Color-coded by the phase system above.

**Log** — Fast capture. 30 seconds max. Physical + emotional + cognitive. Voice option. No forms. Tappable icons, not text fields.

**Insights** — The intelligence layer. "Here's what your data shows." Charts that feel like Apple Health, not Excel. Sharable symptom report for doctor appointments.

**You** — Profile, condition settings, community, privacy controls. Also: "My Story" — a curated timeline of your hormonal journey.

### The Home Screen (Today Tab)

The Today screen is the emotional core of the app. It must feel like opening a message from someone who cares about you.

**Structure (top to bottom):**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Good morning, [Name].              │  ← Warm, personal
│  You're in [Day 18 — Luteal].      │  ← Phase, no hype
│                                     │
│  ┌──────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │  How are you feeling?        │   │  ← One question
│  │  😶 Okay  😩 Rough  ✨ Good  │   │
│  └──────────────────────────────┘   │
│                                     │
│  [What to expect today]             │  ← Honest, not cheery
│  ┌──────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │ Your energy may dip today.   │   │
│  │ This is normal for day 18.   │   │  ← No "you're glowing!"
│  │ Rest is valid. Really.       │   │
│  └──────────────────────────────┘   │
│                                     │
│  [One gentle nudge]                 │  ← Single action
│  ┌──────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │ Log how you slept last night │   │
│  └──────────────────────────────┘   │
│                                     │
│  [Community pulse]                  │
│  342 others are in luteal today.    │  ← Not alone signal
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Log Screen Design

Speed is everything. If logging takes more than 30 seconds, abandonment rate is near 100%.

**The Log Flow:**

1. **Mood** — 5 icons (not words): 😶 💀 😤 😢 ✨ — tap one in 1 second
2. **Physical** — Pre-populated chips for this phase. Day 18 luteal shows: bloating, cramps, fatigue, headache, breast tenderness. Tap any that apply. 3 seconds.
3. **Cognitive** — Brain fog scale 1-5. Single slider. 2 seconds.
4. **Optional voice note** — "Want to say more?" — mic icon. 5-15 seconds if used.
5. **Done.** Total: 10-30 seconds.

**The Anti-Form Philosophy:**  
No text fields in the core logging flow. No dropdowns. No multi-step forms. Every interaction is: see it, tap it, done.

## PSYCHOLOGICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### 1. Validate Before You Educate
Before any information, the app validates the experience first.  
❌ "During luteal phase, progesterone levels drop, causing mood changes."  
✓ "Luteal is genuinely hard. Not in your head. Not dramatic. Hard."  
[Then: "Here's what's happening in your body right now."]

### 2. Name the Darkness Without Amplifying It
Suicidal ideation, PMDD rage, hormonal despair — these are real and common. The app acknowledges them without:
- Triggering crisis responses for normal luteal symptoms
- Pretending they don't exist
- Making users feel pathologized for having them

Implementation: During detected luteal peak, the app says "Some days in this phase can feel really dark. If that's where you are, we're here." — with one tap to crisis resources that appears gently, not urgently.

### 3. Earned Celebration Only
No confetti for logging twice. No badges for "Day 5 Streak!" when day 5 is a luteal hell day.  
Celebration happens for: completing a full cycle of data, surviving a hard PMDD window, hitting a meaningful milestone.  
Celebration tone: **warm and genuine**, not gamified.

### 4. Reduce Friction to Zero at Low-Capacity Moments
Luteal peak is when users need the app most and have the least energy to use it.  
Single-tap check-in must be available from the lock screen.  
When the app detects a hard phase (via patterns), it simplifies the UI automatically — fewer options, softer colors, larger tap targets.

### 5. The Community Without the Chaos
Users found community on Reddit — but Reddit is also exhausting (r/PCOS: "I wish PCOS spaces weren't so exhausting").  
HormonaIQ community is:
- Opt-in, not default
- Anonymous by default
- Phase-segmented ("Talk to others in luteal right now")
- Moderated (zero tolerance for medical misinformation and unsolicited advice)
- Read-only mode always available

### 6. Data Privacy as a First-Class Feature
Not buried in settings. Front and center.
- **Local-first architecture**: data stays on device by default
- **No fertility data sharing** — explicit post-Roe policy, prominently displayed
- **Data export**: user owns their data, one-tap export as PDF for doctors
- **No advertising model** — subscription only, stated upfront

## MICROCOPY GUIDE

### Phase Messages (What the App Says Each Day)

**Follicular (Days 1-10 after period):**
> "Your body is resetting. Energy is building. This is usually a good window."

**Ovulation (Day 14 ±2):**
> "Energy peak for many people. No pressure to be luminous — just notice how you feel."

**Early Luteal (Days 15-20):**
> "The shift is beginning. You might notice small changes — irritability, hunger, fatigue. This is normal."

**Luteal Peak (Days 21-26):**
> "This is often the hardest stretch. Whatever you're feeling is real. Rest counts as doing something."

**Premenstrual (Days 27-28):**
> "Almost through. Your body is about to release a lot. Let it."

**Menstruation:**
> "The hard part is lifting. Rest as long as you need."

### Error States
No "Oops!" No "Something went wrong!" 
> "That didn't work — try again?" ← Calm, direct, human

### Empty States
No "No data yet!"  
> "Nothing here yet. Your first log will show up here." ← Simple, no pressure

### Notifications (Push)
- "Heads up — you're heading into luteal." (Day 14)  
- "Day 21. This week can be hard. We're here." (Luteal peak)  
- "Your period is likely coming in the next 2-3 days." (Not "AF incoming!")  
- "You logged for 7 days straight. That's real data." (Earned, not gamified)

## FEATURE LAYOUT IN UI

### Core Feature Stack (MVP)

**Layer 1 — The Foundation (Week 1 ship)**
- Phase-aware home screen
- Basic 4-option mood log
- Cycle calendar view
- Privacy-first local data storage

**Layer 2 — The Intelligence (Month 2)**
- Pattern recognition after 2 cycles
- Predictive phase warnings
- Doctor-shareable symptom PDF export
- Physical symptom logging (phase-specific chips)

**Layer 3 — The Companion (Month 3)**
- Proactive push notifications (opt-in)
- Community: anonymous phase-segmented forums
- Cognitive tracking (brain fog, concentration)
- Lab value input + trend tracking

**Layer 4 — The Platform (Month 4-6)**
- ADHD medication efficacy tracking overlaid on cycle
- Provider finder (menopause-informed doctors)
- HRT/medication response tracking
- Multi-condition overlay (PMDD + PCOS + ADHD simultaneously)

### The "Log" Button — Always Accessible
The log action is accessible from every screen via a persistent bottom-center button (not in the nav — above it). This is the most important action in the app. It must never be more than one tap away.

## WHAT SUCCESS FEELS LIKE

A user opens the app on day 22 of their cycle. PMDD is hitting hard.

**What they see:**
- Soft lavender background tint (not white, not alarming)
- "Day 22 — You're in the hardest stretch of your cycle."
- "Last month, your symptoms peaked around now too."
- "342 others are tracking luteal today."
- Single question: "How are you feeling right now?"
- One tap. Done.
- Response: "Logged. We've got this week noted. Rest is not failing."

**What they feel:**
Seen. Not alone. Not pathologized. Not celebrated inappropriately.  
Supported by something that understands them.

That is the design goal. Every pixel either achieves it or doesn't.
